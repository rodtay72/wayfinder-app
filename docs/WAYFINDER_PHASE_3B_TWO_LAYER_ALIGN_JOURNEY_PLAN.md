# Wayfinder Phase 3B  -  Two-Layer ALIGN Journey Plan

## Status

**Planning supplement only.** No implementation, code change, schema change, save-payload change, auth change, or deployment change is authorised by this document.

## 1. The core product question Phase 3B resolves

Phase 3 (Your ALIGN Journey) surfaces what the parent is growing across their parenting practice.

But Wayfinder parents typically have more than one child. A parent's ALIGN practice is not uniform across relationships. The same parent capacity  -  staying curious, pausing before reacting, naming the need beneath the behaviour  -  may be genuinely well-practised with one child and chronically challenged with another. The challenge is not because the children are different types of people. It is because each child relationship has a different **dyad activation pattern**  -  a different constellation of child behaviours, parent CAB responses, and recurring misalignment contexts.

Phase 3B resolves the question:

> "How does Wayfinder show both what the parent is growing in themselves overall, and where that growth is activated differently in each relationship  -  without creating child profiles, child comparison, or child personality labels?"

The answer is two layers:

**Layer 1  -  Parent-wide ALIGN Journey**
Purpose: What am I growing in myself across parenting moments?

**Layer 2  -  Child-specific dyad lenses**
Purpose: Where does this alignment challenge appear differently with each child?

Both layers reflect the parent's CAB and regulation capacity. Neither layer is about the child's character, type, or development. The child is always a signal  -  the growth work is always the parent's.

---

## 2. Layer 1  -  Parent-wide ALIGN Journey

### What it is

The parent-wide ALIGN Journey is a single, parent-level view that aggregates reflection evidence across **all children and all entry types** to surface the parent's current overall ALIGN practice context.

It does not belong to one child. It belongs to the parent as a developing practitioner of emotional regulation and alignment.

### What it surfaces

The parent-wide layer surfaces:

- **Current Focus:** Which ALIGN stage the parent's recent reflections most suggest they may be exploring or practising now  -  across all children.
- **Recent Pattern:** A possible recurring CAB pattern noticed across recent reflections  -  not tied to one child.
- **Growth Practice:** A capacity the parent may be currently tending  -  across relationships.
- **Suggested Direction:** One optional next practice direction, based on inferred current focus.

### What it does not do

- It does not score the parent.
- It does not say the parent is at a stage.
- It does not say the parent has completed a stage.
- It does not say one relationship is better or more aligned than another.
- It does not compare children.
- It does not diagnose the parent or the child.

### Language rules

All parent-wide cards use cautious, inviting language:

- "Your recent reflections may suggest..."
- "A possible pattern worth noticing across recent moments..."
- "You may be practising..."
- "One direction that might support this practice..."

Never: "You are at Locate." "You have completed Growth." "Your pattern is urgency."

## 3. Layer 2  -  Dyad-specific lenses

### What they are

Dyad lenses are per-child-relationship summaries that show **where the parent's ALIGN practice is activated differently** in each relationship.

Each dyad lens is linked to one Child ID. It does not build a profile of the child. It reflects how the same parent capacity is challenged in different ways in different relationships.

The framing is always:

> "With this relationship, the alignment challenge may be activating differently."

Not:

> "This child triggers you more."
> "This child needs more regulation."
> "This child is harder."

### What a dyad lens surfaces

- **Activation context:** What kinds of moments, behaviours, or transitions tend to appear in decode entries linked to this Child ID. (Framed as parent-observed contexts  -  not child traits.)
- **CAB pattern in this relationship:** What the parent's Cognition, Affect, and Behaviour pattern may look like in moments linked to this child. (Framed as what the parent noticed in themselves  -  not what the child caused.)
- **Possible need appearing here:** What child need has the parent named most often in decodes for this relationship. (Framed cautiously  -  "a need the parent has been exploring"  -  not a diagnostic conclusion.)
- **Current practice edge in this relationship:** What ALIGN capacity may be most needed in this specific relationship context right now, based on what the parent has noticed.

### What a dyad lens does not do

- It does not create a child profile.
- It does not label the child's personality, attachment style, or developmental pattern.
- It does not compare this child to other children.
- It does not say "this child is avoidant," "this child is controlling," or "this child needs X."
- It does not advance the child relationship to a better "score" based on parent performance.
- It does not suggest the parent has failed in this relationship.

### Relationship to the Relationship Garden

The Relationship Garden (live) already displays per-child relationships as growing plants. Dyad lenses in Phase 3B **complement** the Garden: where the Garden shows a tending-and-growth visual, the dyad lens provides a cautious, reflective summary of what the parent has been noticing and practising within that relationship.

The dyad lens should feel like a quiet companion to each garden pot  -  not a performance report on that pot.

## 4. How the two layers relate

| Dimension | Layer 1  -  Parent-wide | Layer 2  -  Dyad lens |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scope | All children, all entries | One Child ID, entries linked to that child |
| Focus | Parent's overall ALIGN practice | Parent's CAB activation pattern in this relationship |
| Primary question | "What am I growing in myself?" | "Where does this show up differently here?" |
| Evidence base | All decode and activity entries | Decode and activity entries linked to this Child ID only |
| Output | Journey cards for the parent as a whole | Dyad lens card per child in Relationship Garden section |
| What it is never about | Parent scoring, stage unlocking | Child profile, child comparison, child diagnosis |

### Critical rule: the layers share a pathway

Both layers reflect the same parent regulation pathway:

```
Behaviour
 ->  Need
 ->  Parent CAB
 ->  Alignment Check
 ->  Awareness
 ->  Growth
 ->  Navigate / Next Action
```

The parent-wide layer asks: where am I in this pathway overall?

The dyad lens asks: where does this pathway get activated differently in this relationship?

The pathway itself is never about the child. The child's behaviour is always a signal. The parent's CAB is always the subject of the growth work.

## 5. Evidence sufficiency thresholds

Both layers must apply evidence sufficiency thresholds before surfacing any pattern or summary. Generating insights from insufficient evidence risks overclaiming, hallucination, and parent harm.

### Layer 1 thresholds

| Reflection count (all children) | Layer 1 behaviour |
| --- | --- |
| 0 entries | No journey cards. Show gentle invitation only. |
| 1 entry | Single-moment reflection note only. No pattern inference. |
| 2 - 3 entries | Early possible signal only. Highly cautious language required. |
| 4+ entries | Cautious recurring theme may be surfaced. Still use cautious language. |

### Layer 2 thresholds (per Child ID)

| Reflection count (per Child ID) | Dyad lens behaviour |
| --- | --- |
| 0 entries linked to this child | No dyad lens. Invite a first reflection for this relationship. |
| 1 entry | Single-moment observation only. No activation pattern inferred. |
| 2 - 3 entries | Possible early activation context noted. Highly cautious language. |
| 4+ entries | Cautious recurring activation pattern may be surfaced for this relationship. |

### Language rules for insufficient evidence

Do not say:

- "You have not journalled enough."
- "You need more entries to unlock insights."
- "You are behind on reflection."

Prefer:

- "Wayfinder is ready when you are. One small reflection can be a starting point."
- "A few more reflections may help Wayfinder notice a possible pattern more safely."

## 6. Activity recommendation criteria

Both layers may optionally surface an activity direction. Recommendations must be non-commanding, optional, and non-shaming.

### Layer 1  -  Parent-wide recommendation

- Suggest one optional ALIGN-stage-aligned activity direction based on the inferred current overall focus.
- Do not command: "You must do Day 15."
- Do not shame: "You have not practised Navigate."
- Do not advance: recommend a stage that has plausible evidence already, not one the parent has shown no reflection evidence for at all.
- Use language: "You might try a [stage] activity when you are ready." "One direction that may support this practice..."

### Layer 2  -  Dyad-specific recommendation

- If surfaced at all, a dyad lens recommendation must be specific to what has been noticed in this relationship  -  not a generic suggestion.
- Must not compare this recommendation to what was suggested for another child relationship.
- Must not imply one relationship needs "more work" than another.
- Must use "for this relationship" or "in moments like these" framing.
- Must remain optional and non-commanding.

### What recommendations must not do

- Command: "You must try this activity."
- Shame: "You have not completed Navigate."
- Rank: "You are behind in this relationship."
- Over-claim: "This activity will fix the pattern."
- Diagnose: "This suggests an Integrate deficit."
- Compare: "You have done better with Child A than Child B."

## 7. Pitfalls to design against

The following are the highest-risk failure modes for Phase 3B. These must be explicitly designed against, not left to implementation judgment.

### Child profiling by stealth

Dyad lenses must never accumulate into a profile of the child. Even using neutral language like "this child often seems to need control" creates a child type. The framing must always be about what the **parent noticed** or what the **parent explored**  -  not a conclusion about the child.

Incorrect: "Connection is a recurring need for this child."
Correct: "Connection has been a possible need the parent has been exploring in reflections linked to this relationship."

### Child comparison by implication

Two dyad lenses side by side create a natural comparison risk. The design must not allow one lens to visually or verbally imply one relationship is healthier, more advanced, or better tended than another. Avoid relative language across lenses ("more reflections here," "this relationship is at a higher stage"). Each lens must read as complete in itself.

### Pattern overclaiming from few entries

Two entries sharing the same possible need is not a pattern. Surfacing it as a pattern ("a recurring pattern in this relationship is...") could mislead the parent into treating a coincidence as a diagnosis. The evidence thresholds in Section 5 must be treated as hard gates, not soft guidelines.

### Parent stage assignment

Cards that say "You are at Locate" are forbidden. Cards that say "Locate appears to be your current focus based on recent reflections" remain cautious. The distinction matters and must be preserved in every card heading, label, and body copy.

### Repair-as-failure framing

The Navigate / repair section of ALIGN must never be framed as evidence that the relationship "failed." A Navigate or repair reflection is evidence of parental growth  -  the capacity to return, reconnect, and practise again. Repair must be presented as an ALIGN strength, not a problem.

## 8. Technical constraints

No implementation is authorised by this document. However, the following constraints must shape any future implementation brief.

### Must not change

- Supabase auth, RLS, or SQL schema
- API routes or serverless functions (unless a new function is separately reviewed and approved)
- `supabase.js`
- `ensure_profile` or email verification gate
- Parent ID / Child ID generation
- Journal save / read behaviour (`DB.saveEntry`, `DB.getEntries`, `DB.getAllDyads`, `DB.saveDyad`)
- Deployment config (`vercel.json`, environment variables)

### Must not introduce

- New database tables without a separate schema/RLS review document
- Browser-side AI API keys
- Browser-side calls to external AI models
- New save payload fields without explicit Rodney approval of the field shape, meaning, and backward compatibility plan
- Child names in any UI layer (normal or debug)
- Parent email in any UI layer (normal)
- Supabase UUIDs in normal UI

### Must derive from already-loaded data

Any Journey or dyad lens derivation must use already-loaded `entries` and `dyads` (from existing `DB.getEntries` and `DB.getAllDyads` calls). No new database calls should be required for the MVP of this feature.

### Save-payload fields are unresolved

Fields such as `alignStage`, `alignStagesExplored`, `primaryStageExplored`, and `activityTag` have been discussed in parent planning documents as future candidates. They are **not approved as Phase 3B design** in this document. Their format, derivation rules, backward compatibility approach, and save-path changes must be agreed separately before any implementation brief includes them as accepted design.

## 9. Future component shape (planning sketch)

This section describes the intended product components in conceptual terms only. Component names, prop shapes, and code structure are subject to implementation review.

### Your ALIGN Journey section (Layer 1)

A dashboard section containing parent-wide Journey cards. Cards derive from all available entries regardless of child. Section placement: after Relationship Garden, before or beside Decode a Moment and Past Activities.

Planned cards:

| Card | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Current Focus | Which ALIGN stage may be the parent's current practice context |
| Recent Pattern | A possible recurring CAB theme across recent reflections |
| Growth Practice | A capacity the parent may be currently tending |
| Suggested Direction | One optional next practice direction |

Empty state behaviour: If reflection evidence is below the minimum threshold, show a single gentle invitation card in place of all four cards.

### Dyad lens component (Layer 2)

A per-child summary that appears alongside or within each Relationship Garden pot  -  not as a separate section, but as a contextual expansion of each existing garden entry.

Each dyad lens shows:

| Element | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Activation context note | What kinds of parenting moments have appeared in this relationship's reflections |
| Parent CAB pattern here | What the parent has noticed in themselves during moments linked to this child |
| Possible need explored | What need the parent has been exploring most in decodes for this relationship |
| Practice edge in this relationship | What capacity may be most worth tending in this specific relationship context |

The dyad lens must not be visible if the evidence threshold for that Child ID has not been met.

### Section and component rules

- Both the Journey section and dyad lens must be read-only.
- Both must fail gracefully (empty states, no errors) if data is unavailable.
- Both must use cautious language throughout (see Section 6 language rules).
- Both must be dismissible or collapsible so parents are not overwhelmed.
- Neither must interfere with existing dashboard loading, Journal Trail, Decode a Moment, or Relationship Garden behaviour.

## 11. Open questions for Rodney

1. **Dyad lens placement:** Should each dyad lens appear as a card inside the Relationship Garden section (one per garden pot), or as a separate expandable section below the garden?

2. **Dyad lens visibility threshold:** Should a dyad lens appear as soon as there is 1 entry linked to a Child ID (as a single-moment note), or only at 2+ entries?

3. **Layer 1 scope:** Should the parent-wide Journey cards aggregate across all children without exception, or should parents be able to filter Layer 1 to a specific child when they want to focus?

4. **Suggested Direction scoping:** Should the Layer 1 Suggested Direction recommend an activity for the parent generally, or should it factor in which child relationship has the most recent reflection activity?

5. **Repair framing:** Should Navigate/repair reflection evidence be surfaced with a specific acknowledgement ("you have shown willingness to repair in this relationship") or remain implicit in the practice language?

6. **Save-payload decision:** Should Phase 3B implementation include adding `alignStage` tags to activity journal saves and `alignStagesExplored` to decode saves  -  or should derivation rely entirely on existing `phase`, nested `align{}` sections, and entry type without new payload fields?

7. **Evidence recency:** Should the evidence window for Current Focus and Recent Pattern be time-based (e.g. last 30 days), entry-count-based (e.g. last 5 entries), or a combination?

8. **Multi-child display cap:** If a parent has many children, should the dyad lens section show all child relationships or cap at a visible number with a "see more" option?

9. **MVP scope:** Is the full two-layer design (all Journey cards + all dyad lenses) required for Phase 3B release, or is a reduced MVP (e.g. Layer 1 only, with dyad lenses deferred to Phase 3C) acceptable?

10. **Counsellor visibility:** When a counsellor views a parent in a future Phase 4 counsellor view, should they see both layers, or only Layer 2 dyad lenses as more clinically relevant?
